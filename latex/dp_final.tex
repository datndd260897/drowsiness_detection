Between 2017 and 2021, 17.6\% of all ftal crashes involved drowsy driving~\cite{tefft2024drowsy}. 
This issue keeps continuing today; As of Novermber 2025, the Colorado Sate Patrol reported 627 related incidents~\cite{patla2025drowsy}, 
and A 2024 survey indicate that 25 \% of drivers admitted to having engaged in drowsy driving within the past 30 days~\cite{AAA2024TSCI}.

In order to successfully prevent traffic accidents, detecting drowsiness is a fundamental and valuable requirement. 
Therefore, we were looking to develop a program capable of predicting when a driver may become drowsy and immediately notifying them to avoid serious accidents on the road. 
To achieve this goal, we need to track the driver's eye-opening and eye-closing patterns to determine whether the driver is in a drowsy-driving state. 
To address this problem, we applied deep learning methods, which will be explained in the following chapters.

Inspired by Hassan et al.~\cite{Hassan2025}, this project adopts a simpler approach to develop an effective eye-state classification system (open vs.\ closed) based on a deep learning model trained on the MRL dataset, while applying the best-performing model to provide real-time eye-state feedback.
